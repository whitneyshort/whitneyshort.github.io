Outreach

One of the most rewarding and exciting experiences of advancing in my education has been the increasing opportunity to transition from the mentee to the mentor role. My mentors have helped shape my own path, and the ability to give back by helping to build up the future generation of scientists and citizens is something I value strongly and prioritize.

Activities

May 2026–Present · University of Chicago Department of Chemistry

Math Bootcamp Organizer

Each year, the Department of Chemistry facilitates a graduate-student organized Math Bootcamp for incoming Ph.D. students to brush up on the requisite math skills for their graduate level physical chemistry classes. The goal of the program is to put students with potentially less-rigorous or recent math backgrounds on a level playing field and to prep specific techniques and concepts required for the work in their first quarter classes. As a member of the organizing committee, I am responsible for curriculum development, teacher recruitment, and lesson feedback to session teachers, as well as financial and organizational duties associated with executing the two-week long course during orientation.

Graduate Academic outreach
March 2026–Present · University of Chicago Laboratory School

WiSTEM Mentorship Program Mentor

Through this program initiated through a partnership with the Laboratory School's WiSTEM organization and UChicago's WiC+ (Women in Chemistry +) organization, I am paired with a female high school student who has strong interest in math and chemistry. I mentor her through biweekly meetings and program-wide events such as panels and mixers. I aim to encourage my mentee to pursue a path in STEM and provide my perspective on my experiences as a woman in STEM from my time in college and now in my Ph.D.

9-12 Mentorship
February 2026–Present · Wendell Phillips Academy High School

Geometry and Trigonometry & Algebra II Tutor

I tutor math weekly at Wendell Phillips Academy High School during class time. The goal of this program is to help students with their math during their lesson time, while they are doing the problems in school. Most students at this high school face food, housing, and other instabilities outside of school; this program is designed to meet them in the classroom to build up their basic math and reasoning skills to allow them to build new knowledge and complete their work independently rather than being in a perpetual catch-up phase.

9-12 Tutoring
November 2025–Present · Physical Sciences Division Pen Pals Program

STEM Student Pen Pal

Throughout the past academic year, I have corresponded with a fifth grade student at St. Thomas the Apostle Catholic school. Within my letters, I have talked about what it is like to be a scientist, what made me excited about chemistry when I was earlier in my education, and some obstacles I've faced along my path. My pen pal has written about her career interests and hobbies and her favorite things to learn in school; a primary goal of the program is to connect our pen pal's interests with how they might be able to pursue them via a STEM education and get them excited about science.

October 2025 · South Side Science Festival

Scientist Volunteer

This yearly event organizes dozens of science demos targeted at kids of all ages to show them science and research in a fun and exciting format. My role involved helping set up the event day-of and facilitate demos and attractions within the festival.
